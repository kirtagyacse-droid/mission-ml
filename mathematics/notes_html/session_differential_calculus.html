01 · Derivative as Tangent Slope

The derivative $f'(x)$ measures the instantaneous rate of change of a function $f(x)$ at point $x$, geometrically representing the slope of the tangent line to the function at that point.

02 · Fundamental Rules of Differentiation

Core rules of differentiation:

  • Power Rule: $d(x^n)/dx = n x^{n-1}$.
  • Product Rule: $d(uv)/dx = u'v + uv'$.
  • Quotient Rule: $d(u/v)/dx = (u'v - uv') / v^2$.

03 · The Chain Rule

Essential for backpropagation in neural networks. It computes the derivative of composite functions:

Chain Rule
dy/dx = (dy/du) · (du/dx)

04 · Partial Derivatives

When a function depends on multiple variables, the partial derivative $\partial f / \partial x_i$ measures the rate of change with respect to $x_i$ while holding all other variables constant.

05 · The Gradient Vector

The **gradient** $ abla f(\mathbf{x})$ is a vector containing all first-order partial derivatives of a scalar function:

Gradient Vector
∇f(x) = [ ∂f/∂x₁, ∂f/∂x₂, ..., ∂f/∂x_n ]ᵀ

06 · The Jacobian Matrix

For a vector-valued function $\mathbf{f}: \mathbb{R}^n o \mathbb{R}^m$, the **Jacobian** $J$ is a matrix of all first-order partial derivatives, representing the local linear approximation of the function.

07 · The Hessian Matrix

The **Hessian** $H$ is a square matrix of second-order partial derivatives of a scalar function, describing the local curvature (concavity/convexity) of the function:

Hessian Matrix Elements
H_(i,j) = ∂²f / (∂x_i · ∂x_j)

08 · Taylor Series Approximation

A representation of a function as an infinite sum of terms calculated from the values of its derivatives at a single point, enabling us to approximate complex functions locally.

09 · Quick Reference Card

🚀 Calculus Cheat Sheet

  • Gradient: Vector of first derivatives, points to steepest ascent.
  • Jacobian: Matrix of first derivatives for vector functions.
  • Hessian: Matrix of second derivatives, determines local curvature.
