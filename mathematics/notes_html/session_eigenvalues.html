01 · The Eigenvalue Equation (Av = λv)

An eigenvector of a square matrix $A$ is a non-zero vector $\mathbf{v}$ that does not change direction when multiplied by $A$. It is only scaled by a factor called the eigenvalue ($\lambda$).

Eigen Equation
A · v = λ · v

02 · The Characteristic Equation

To find eigenvalues, we rewrite the equation as $(A - \lambda I)\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{0}$. Since $\mathbf{v}$ is non-zero, the matrix $(A - \lambda I)$ must be singular:

Characteristic Equation
det(A − λI) = 0

Solving this polynomial equation yields the eigenvalues $\lambda$.

03 · Finding Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Once we solve for $\lambda$, we plug each eigenvalue back into $(A - \lambda I)\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{0}$ and solve for the corresponding eigenvector $\mathbf{v}$ (which lies in the null space of $A - \lambda I$).

04 · Geometric Intuition of Eigenvalues

Linear transformations typically rotate and scale vectors. Eigenvectors are the **axes of the transformation** — vectors along these axes do not rotate, they only stretch or shrink by the factor $\lambda$.

05 · Algebraic vs Geometric Multiplicity

  • Algebraic Multiplicity: How many times an eigenvalue appears as a root of the characteristic polynomial.
  • Geometric Multiplicity: The number of linearly independent eigenvectors associated with that eigenvalue.

06 · Properties of Eigenvalues

For any $n imes n$ matrix $A$:

  • Sum of Eigenvalues: Equals the **trace** of the matrix (sum of diagonal entries).
  • Product of Eigenvalues: Equals the **determinant** of the matrix.

07 · Eigenvalues of Special Matrices

  • Symmetric Matrices: Always have real eigenvalues, and their eigenvectors are orthogonal.
  • Triangular Matrices: Eigenvalues are simply the entries on the main diagonal.

08 · Python Calculation of Eigenvalues

import numpy as np

A = np.array([[2, 1], [1, 2]])
eigenvalues, eigenvectors = np.linalg.eig(A)

print("Eigenvalues:", eigenvalues)
print("Eigenvectors:
", eigenvectors)

09 · Quick Reference Card

🚀 Eigenvalues Cheat Sheet

  • Equation: $A\mathbf{v} = \lambda \mathbf{v}$.
  • Trace: $\text{Tr}(A) = \sum \lambda_i$.
  • Determinant: $\det(A) = \prod \lambda_i$.
